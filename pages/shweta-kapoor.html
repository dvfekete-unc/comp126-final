Shweta Kapoor

Biography

Shweta Kapoor is an assistant professor at Emory University. Her specificalized area is psychiatry and behavioral sciences. Her interested areas are chronic pain, severe, and persistent mental illness. She has worked on and continues to do research with those areas of interest with a specific emphasis on pain response. Kapoor started her work at the Emory- Grady psychology internship program. She then continued her involvement with clinical research in her fellowship year. She has earned her MBBS, done residency at University of Texas Health Science Center at Houston, residency at Emory University School of Medicine, earned her PhD in clinical health psychology, and her postdoctoral fellowship in clinical psychology.

Shweta Kappor was born in India. She speaks Hindi, Punjabi, and English. She earned her MBBS at Kasturba Medical College, located in Manipal, India. Later, she finished her residency at the University of Texas at Houston, and at Emory University School of Medicine, where she continued her involvement with clinical research. After she had completed her residency, she earned a PhD from the University of Alabama. Currently, Kapoor is an assistant professor at Emory University.

Research

Her specificalized area is psychiatry and behavioral sciences. Her interested areas are chronic pain, severe, and persistent mental illness with emphasis on pain response. In one of her recent published research work, Kapoor and her team investigated the interactive effect of the serotonin transporter genotype and drug use on suicidal behaviors in patients with bipolar disorder, abbreviated as BD (Baylor College, n.d.). The results were: 1) Based on drug use, the short and long allele had different effects. 2) for BD patients with little to no drug use, the short allele was the risk of suicidal behavior. 3) for BD patients with a higher amount of drug use, the long allele was the risk for suicidal behavior (Roane et al., 2019).
